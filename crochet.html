Bumblebee Beluga Red Panda Turtle

-Bumblebee-

Scientific name: Bombus terrestris

Conservation status: Vulnerable

Geographical location: Found primarily in the Northern Hemisphere, also found in South America

Description: Have round bodies covered in soft hair and aposematic colouration, often consisting of contrasting bands of colour

Current population: Unidentified

Reasons for endangerment: Habitat loss, climate change, pesticide use, and disease

-Beluga-

Scientific name: Delphinapterus leucas

Geographical location: Primarily inhabit the Arctic and subarctic waters of the Northern Hemisphere

Description: White in colour and possesses a protuberance at the front of its head which houses an echolocation organ.

Current population: 150,000 - 200,000

Reasons for endangerment: Climate change, pollution, habitat loss, and hunting

-Red Panda-

Scientific name: Ailurus fulgens

Geographical location: Native to the Eastern Himalayas and Southwestern China

Description: Mainly red or orange-brown with a black belly and legs

Current population: Less than 10 000

Reasons for endangerment: Habitat loss, hunting, and poaching

-Leatherback Turtle-

Scientific name: Dermochelys coriacea

Geographical location: Located in the Atlantic, Pacific, and Indian Oceans

Description: Has leather-like skin and a flexible carapace that covered by oily flesh

Current population: 2,300 adult females

Reasons for endangerment: Fisheries bycatch, direct harvest of eggs and meat, and habitat degradation
